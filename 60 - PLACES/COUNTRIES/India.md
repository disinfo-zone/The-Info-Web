India is a federal parliamentary republic in South Asia, the world's most populous country, bordering [[Pakistan]], [[China]], Bangladesh, Bhutan, Nepal, and Myanmar, with coastline on the Indian Ocean. India gained independence from British colonial rule on August 15, 1947, under Prime Minister [[Jawaharlal Nehru]] and has been the world's largest democracy since. India pursued a foreign policy of Non-Alignment during the [[Cold War]], maintaining relationships with both the [[United States]] and the [[Soviet Union]], though it tilted toward the Soviet side following the 1971 Indo-Pakistani War.[^1]

### CIA Operations and Cold War

The [[Central Intelligence Agency]] conducted significant covert operations in India during the Cold War, particularly targeting India's communist parties and left-wing organizations. The CIA provided covert funding to centrist and right-wing Indian political parties in the 1950s and 1960s. CIA surveillance of Indian scientific and military programs was also extensive, given India's nuclear ambitions (India conducted its first nuclear test in May 1974) and its Soviet military equipment acquisitions.[^1]

India's intelligence service, the [[Research and Analysis Wing]] (RAW, established 1968), maintained its own foreign intelligence operations and maintained complex relationships with Western services. RAW's primary operational focus was on [[Pakistan]], [[China]], and South Asian neighbors, but its relationship with the CIA evolved through the Cold War and post-Cold War periods.[^2]

### Arms Sales and Brokers

India was a significant market for French military aircraft, particularly Mirage jets. Arms broker [[Asaf Ali]], who appears in this vault's Israel-related arms dealing subjects, is documented as having brokered the sale of forty-nine Mirage 2000s to India through his relationships with Dassault and various government intermediaries. The arms sales networks that supplied India during the Cold War and post-Cold War period overlapped with the same broker networks documented in connection with Israeli arms exports, BCCI financial flows, and the wider arms trade ecosystem.[^2]

### BCCI

The [[Bank of Credit and Commerce International]] maintained a significant Indian presence, with branches across major Indian cities. BCCI's Indian operations were supervised by India's Reserve Bank, which - like the [[Bank of England]] - was later criticized for inadequate oversight. The BCCI connections to Indian government and business figures were examined in the aftermath of the bank's 1991 collapse. India's large non-resident community in the Gulf states, particularly the [[United Arab Emirates]] and [[Abu Dhabi]], provided a natural customer base for BCCI's Gulf operations.[^1]

### Nuclear Program

India's nuclear weapons program produced its first test ("Smiling Buddha") on May 18, 1974, at Pokhran in Rajasthan. India subsequently developed a complete nuclear weapons capability and conducted five additional tests in May 1998 ("Operation Shakti"). India's nuclear program existed in parallel with Pakistan's [[A.Q. Khan]]-led nuclear weapons development, which the CIA monitored with concern throughout the 1980s and 1990s. The two programs created the South Asian nuclear rivalry that remains a defining feature of the region's security environment.[^1]
