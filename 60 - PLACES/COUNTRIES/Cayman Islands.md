The Cayman Islands is a British Overseas Territory in the western Caribbean Sea, comprising Grand Cayman, Cayman Brac, and Little Cayman, with its capital at George Town. With no corporate or income tax, strict banking secrecy laws, and a British legal framework providing institutional credibility, the Cayman Islands became one of the world's largest offshore financial centers from the 1970s onward. Thousands of banks, investment funds, and shell companies registered in the Caymans during the period covered by this vault - many with no physical presence on the islands - using the jurisdiction as a conduit for financial flows that required anonymity or favorable tax treatment.[^1]

### BCCI

The [[Bank of Credit and Commerce International]] was incorporated in the Cayman Islands - registered in Luxembourg but with its ultimate corporate structure anchored in Cayman secrecy. BCCI's Cayman incorporation allowed its true ownership and financial condition to be shielded from effective regulatory scrutiny by the [[Bank of England]] or other national supervisors. The [[Price Waterhouse]] auditing firm, which audited BCCI's Cayman-registered entities, was later found to have missed or ignored massive fraud. When BCCI was seized by regulators in 66 countries simultaneously on July 5, 1991, Cayman-registered entities held billions in assets that became subject to insolvency proceedings conducted under Cayman law.[^2]

### CIA and Iran-Contra Financial Flows

The CIA used Cayman-registered entities as part of its operational financial infrastructure throughout the 1970s and 1980s. The [[Southern Air Transport]] CIA-front airline maintained financial accounts through Cayman-registered entities. The Contra resupply network - the "Enterprise" organized by [[Oliver North]], [[Richard Secord]], and [[Albert Hakim]] - routed funds through accounts in the [[Switzerland|Swiss]] financial system and Cayman-registered entities before disbursement to weapons suppliers and logistics operators.[^2]

[[Adnan Khashoggi]]'s financial transactions in the Iran-Contra arms-for-hostages pipeline used Cayman accounts alongside [[Switzerland|Swiss]] accounts and accounts in the [[Bahamas]] to obscure the origins and destinations of funds. [[Earl Brian]]'s [[Hadron]] company and associated entities used Cayman-registered holding structures during the [[Inslaw Affair|Inslaw/PROMIS]] period.[^1]

### Drug Money Laundering

The Cayman Islands was identified in multiple DEA and congressional investigations as a significant conduit for drug trafficking proceeds during the 1980s. Colombian [[Medellin Cartel]] proceeds, [[Cali Cartel]] funds, and Contra-linked drug revenues moved through Cayman accounts. The proximity to [[Panama]] - where [[Manuel Noriega]]'s government also facilitated money laundering through its banking system - made the Cayman Islands part of an interconnected Caribbean financial architecture used by intelligence agencies, drug traffickers, and arms dealers during the period.[^2]
