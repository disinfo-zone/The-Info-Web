Vorherrschaft Division (VSD) is a U.S.-based neo-Nazi accelerationist organization that operates primarily through [[Telegram]], named from the German word for "domination" (as in global racial domination). The group peaked at approximately 200 subscribers on Telegram and is documented as an organizational ally of [[Feuerkrieg Division]] and [[InJekt Division]] within the [[United Acceleration Front]] coalition network. The most serious publicly documented violence connected to a Vorherrschaft Division participant was the 2020 hospital bombing plot by [[Timothy Wilson]], who was killed by the [[FBI]] during an attempted arrest in Belton, Missouri in March 2020.[^1]

### Ideology and Structure

VSD operated in the [[Siege Culture]] tradition, endorsing leaderless resistance and the glorification of mass violence as a catalyst for racial civilizational collapse. The [[ADL]] glossary characterizes it as a white supremacist group that has "embraced Siege culture." It operated without a publicly identified formal leadership structure. Its Telegram channel served as a distribution node for accelerationist propaganda materials. A public alliance between VSD and Feuerkrieg Division was announced on Telegram before FKD's 2020 dissolution; the two groups circulated joint propaganda materials. When FKD re-emerged in May 2021, it continued working with VSD and InJekt Division in building the [[United Acceleration Front]].[^2]

The [[Tracking Terrorism]] (TRAC) interconnectivity analysis documented VSD alongside FKD, InJekt Division, and [[Totenwaffen]] as the four primary nodes in this coalition network, all operating through Terrorgram-adjacent Telegram channels.[^2]

### Timothy Wilson (2020)

[[Timothy Wilson]], 36, of Belton, Missouri, was active in VSD's public Telegram channel under the handle "Werwolfe 84" and was simultaneously active in [[National Socialist Movement]] (NSM) channels. Wilson was the subject of a months-long FBI terrorism investigation that revealed him to be, in the FBI's characterization, "a potentially violent extremist, motivated by racial, religious, and anti-government animus." In his Telegram posts, Wilson expressed admiration for [[The Order]], the 1983-1984 neo-Nazi criminal organization.

During the early COVID-19 pandemic, Wilson settled on bombing a hospital in the Belton area, writing on Telegram that he believed the pandemic was being used by the government as "an excuse to destroy our people." FBI agents attempted to arrest Wilson at his residence on March 24, 2020. Wilson opened fire on agents, who returned fire; Wilson was killed at the scene. No bomb device was recovered, but the investigation established operational planning. Because Wilson died during the arrest attempt, no criminal prosecution resulted, and the full scope of his VSD activity was not publicly documented in any court record.[^3]

### Status

The VSD Telegram channel operated at least through 2022. No VSD-specific proscription, formal terrorist designation, or criminal prosecution targeting the organization as an entity has been identified in open sources as of May 2026. Its small subscriber base and the death of its most documented violent member limited its operational significance relative to FKD and InJekt Division, but its documented coalition role in the UAF structure makes it a confirmed node in the AWD-to-Terrorgram organizational pipeline.[^1]
