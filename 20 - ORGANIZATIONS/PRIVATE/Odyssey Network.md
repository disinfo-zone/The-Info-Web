### Overview

The Odyssey Network was a sophisticated child trafficking and prostitution operation based in Dallas, Texas, during the 1970s. Led by [[John David Norman]], the network operated as part of a larger international system for the exploitation of children, coordinating with similar operations across the United States and Europe.[^1]

### Organization and Operations

The network, also referred to as "The Delta Project," operated through a system of "sponsors" who would provide housing and support for young boys in exchange for sexual access. These sponsors were connected through newsletters and personal contacts, creating an interlocking web of vice that spanned multiple states.[^1]

[[John David Norman]] managed the operation from Dallas while serving a prison sentence, with his closest associate [[Phillip Paske]] carrying out day-to-day operations. The network used coded language and front organizations to facilitate their activities, including educational foundations and religious charities.[^1]

### Connection to Other Networks

The Odyssey Network was closely linked to other trafficking operations, including the [[Hermes]] newsletter serving as a conduit for engaging children in pornographic modeling and prostitution, the [[Educational Foundation for Youth]] as an Illinois-based front organization deriving tax-exempt status as a subsidiary of offshore companies, and the North Fox Island operation as a Michigan-based summer camp used as a front for child pornography production.[^2]

### International Reach

The network maintained connections with European operations, particularly through [[Francis Shelden]]'s later activities in Amsterdam. Shelden, who fled Michigan to avoid prosecution, later established [[PAN Magazine]] and worked with [[John Stamford]]'s [[Spartacus Network]], effectively creating a European counterpart to the Odyssey Network.[^3]

### Investigation and Shutdown

The network came under investigation in 1977 following arrests in Chicago that revealed connections to Norman's operation. The investigation uncovered the systematic nature of the trafficking operation, including the use of coded communications and the exploitation of vulnerable children through foster care and juvenile systems.[^1]
