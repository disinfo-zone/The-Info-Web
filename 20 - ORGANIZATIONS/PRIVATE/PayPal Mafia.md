The PayPal Mafia is the informal network of founders and early executives of [[PayPal]] who, after the company's 2002 sale to [[eBay]] for 1.5 billion dollars, dispersed to found or lead much of the subsequent generation of [[Silicon Valley]] companies. The cohort acquired the "Mafia" label as a recognizable social and commercial network whose members sit on each other's boards, invest in each other's ventures, and convene, through [[Dialog]], in the same off-the-record settings. Six members of the network appear on the leaked 2026 Dialog retreat roster, and the documented commercial, convening, ideological, and political infrastructure of the [[Thiel Influence Network]] runs substantially through the PayPal Mafia cohort.[^1][^2][^3]

### The PayPal Period (1998-2002)

[[Peter Thiel]] founded Confinity in 1998 to develop security software for handheld devices, pivoting into the person-to-person payments product that became PayPal. Confinity merged with [[Elon Musk]]'s X.com in 2000 to form PayPal, with Thiel as chief executive and Musk as the largest shareholder. The merger resolved a direct competition between the two companies that had produced a mutually destructive customer-acquisition war. The company went public in February 2002 and was acquired by eBay in October 2002 for 1.5 billion dollars. The exit dispersed a cohort of founders and early engineers, each capitalized at levels that permitted the founding of the subsequent generation of consumer-internet, payments, and defense-technology companies.[^1][^3]

The anti-fraud data architecture that Thiel and [[Max Levchin]] built at PayPal was the technical foundation of the subsequent Palantir Technologies government-data-fusion platform. The PayPal system integrated credit-card transaction data, user-behavior signals, and network-graph analytics to identify fraudulent accounts in real time. The same architecture, applied to intelligence and law-enforcement data sources, became the [[Palantir Gotham|Palantir Gotham]] ontology approach. The documented lineage from PayPal fraud detection to Palantir government data fusion is the technical continuity between the two companies.[^1][^3]

### The Principal Members and Their Subsequent Ventures

Max Levchin was PayPal's chief technology officer and cofounder. Levchin subsequently founded [[Affirm]] (the buy-now-pay-later lender, taken public in 2021), served on the boards of [[Yelp]] and [[Evernote]], and established himself as the technical conscience of the PayPal Mafia cohort. Levchin's anti-fraud architecture patent portfolio underlies portions of the subsequent payments-technology industry.

[[Reid Hoffman]] served as executive vice president and a founding board member, responsible for external relations and partnerships. Hoffman subsequently founded [[LinkedIn]] (2002, before the eBay sale closed), took it public in 2011, sold it to [[Microsoft]] for 26.2 billion dollars in 2016, and joined [[Greylock Partners|Greylock]] as a general partner in 2009. Hoffman's politics diverge from the Thiel-Musk Republican alignment: he has been a prominent [[Democratic Party|Democratic]] donor supporting [[Joe Biden]] and [[Kamala Harris]]. His documented [[Jeffrey Epstein|Epstein]] relationship (the image-rehabilitation apology, the 2015 dinner) places a Democratic-aligned PayPal Mafia principal inside the same post-conviction Epstein network as the Republican-aligned Thiel.[^2]

Elon Musk founded X.com (the precursor to PayPal) and was PayPal's largest shareholder before and after the Confinity merger. Musk subsequently founded [[SpaceX]] (2002), joined [[Tesla, Inc.|Tesla]] as lead investor and chairman in 2004 (later chief executive), and acquired [[X (social media)|X (formerly Twitter)]] in 2022. Musk's documented [[Vladimir Putin|Putin]] contact channel (since late 2022, per the [[Wall Street Journal]]), the [[Jonathan Buma|Buma]]-documented GRU targeting of Musk, the [[Igor Kurganov|Kurganov]] infiltration, and the [[NLRB Data Exfiltration|NLRB data exfiltration]] through [[Department of Government Efficiency|DOGE]] all place Musk at the intersection of the PayPal Mafia commercial network and the documented Russian-intelligence and federal-data-access questions.[^2][^4]

[[David Sacks]] served as chief operating officer. Sacks subsequently founded [[Yammer]] (acquired by Microsoft in 2012), produced the film *[[Thank You for Smoking|Thank You for Smoking]]*, and served in the second [[Donald Trump|Trump]] administration as the White House [[AI and cryptocurrency|AI and cryptocurrency]] czar, formally the Special Advisor on Artificial Intelligence and Cryptocurrency. Sacks hosted the [[All-In Podcast|All-In Podcast]], which became a documented media channel for the PayPal Mafia cohort's political positions during the 2022-2025 period.[^5]

[[Ken Howery]] served as chief financial officer and subsequently cofounded [[Founders Fund]] with Thiel and [[Luke Nosek]]. Howery served as the [[United States Ambassador to Sweden|U.S. Ambassador to Sweden]] during the first Trump administration (2019-2021), receiving a formal diplomatic appointment through the Thiel-Trump relationship.

Luke Nosek was a founding team member and subsequently a Founders Fund partner. Nosek sits on the SpaceX board, maintaining a formal board role at a Musk company across the post-PayPal period.

[[Roelof Botha]] served as chief financial officer and subsequently joined [[Sequoia Capital]] as a partner. Botha's Sequoia portfolio includes [[YouTube]] (where he facilitated the Google acquisition), [[Square]] (the payments company, now [[Block (company)|Block]]), and [[Unity Technologies]]. Botha represents the PayPal Mafia cohort's penetration of the broader venture-capital establishment through Sequoia, the firm historically dominant in Silicon Valley venture investing.

[[Keith Rabois]] served in an executive role and subsequently held positions at Founders Fund, [[OpenStore]], and PayPal-adjacent ventures. Rabois has been a documented intermediary between the PayPal Mafia cohort and the broader [[Khosla Ventures|Khosla Ventures]] and Founders Fund networks.

[[Jeremy Stoppelman]] subsequently cofounded Yelp. [[Chad Hurley]], [[Steve Chen]], and [[Jawed Karim]] were early PayPal engineers who subsequently cofounded YouTube, which [[Google]] acquired for 1.65 billion dollars in 2006.[^1][^2]

### The 2016 Trump Campaign and Mercer Money

The PayPal Mafia cohort's political activity converged on the 2016 Trump campaign through Thiel's endorsement and the [[Mercer family|Mercer family]] connection. The Mercers funded [[Breitbart|Breitbart News]] (through which [[Steve Bannon|Steve Bannon]] operated), [[Cambridge Analytica|Cambridge Analytica]] (the data-targeting firm), and the pro-Trump super PACs. Thiel spoke at the 2016 Republican National Convention, donated over one million dollars to Trump-supporting efforts, and served on the transition executive committee. The documented convergence of the Thiel commercial network ([[Palantir Technologies|Palantir]], Founders Fund) with the Mercer political network (Breitbart, Cambridge Analytica) through the Trump campaign is the political instance of the PayPal Mafia cohort's federal-power exercise.[^1][^3]

The cohort's internal political divergence has been documented across the 2016-2025 period. Hoffman has been the principal Democratic-aligned member, funding Democratic candidates and opposing Trump. Musk moved from apolitical to Republican-aligned across 2022-2024, acquiring X and shifting its content moderation toward the Republican position. Sacks moved into the formal Trump administration. Thiel funded the [[JD Vance]] and [[Blake Masters]] Senate campaigns and brokered the Vance vice-presidential selection. The documented divergence within the cohort has not fractured the commercial and convening relationships: Hoffman appears on the leaked 2026 Dialog roster alongside Thiel and Musk, placing the Democratic-aligned and Republican-aligned principals in the same off-the-record setting.[^1][^2]
