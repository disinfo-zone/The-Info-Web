Coleman Thomas Blevins (born approximately 1989, Kerrville, Texas) is the founder of [[InJekt Division]], a neo-Nazi accelerationist [[Telegram]] organization he established in February 2021. He operated under the alias "Korb" (also "Kørbe") and served as one of two members of the group's self-described inner circle, the "Shock Waffen Twins." Blevins was arrested in May 2021 after posting a mass-shooting threat targeting a [[Kerrville]] Walmart on social media, and was sentenced in May 2024 to 60 months in federal prison following a guilty plea to one count of felon-in-possession-of-a-firearm in the [[Western District of Texas]].[^1]

### InJekt Division Founding

Blevins founded InJekt Division in February 2021. Using the Telegram alias Korb, he developed the group's ideology (which he and members called "[[clerical fascism]]," a blend of accelerationist national socialism, religious framing, and misogyny) and its signature slogan, "pray for rain," referencing a desired nuclear event that would collapse civilization. During at least one far-right podcast, a person using the name Korb identified himself as the president of the organization. Blevins appeared in promotional photographs holding firearms alongside InJekt Division imagery.[^2]

In a February 2021 Telegram post, Blevins described the inner circle as led by the "Shock Waffen Twins" (SWT), two individuals who directed all group activity and membership decisions. Photographs in InJekt Division's Telegram channel confirmed Blevins as one of the two; the second SWT member's identity was never publicly confirmed in any court document or named press account.[^2]

[[Feuerkrieg Division]] re-emerged on Telegram in May 2021 specifically to promote and network with InJekt Division, publicly announcing a strategic partnership. InJekt Division and FKD together served as the primary organizing infrastructure for the [[United Acceleration Front]], one of two militant accelerationist Telegram coalitions documented by the [[Middlebury Institute CTEC]] and [[Tech Against Terrorism]] in their 2022 joint report on neo-fascist coalition-building.[^3]

### Arrest and Federal Case

On May 27, 2021, Blevins posted publicly to social media: "I'm going to Walmart," followed by a photograph of himself holding a black AR-15 rifle, and then the message, "I have no problem spending the rest of my life in prison for Shootings [sic] up Walmart." Kerr County, Texas authorities executed a search warrant on May 28, 2021. Officers found 556 rounds of ammunition in Blevins's apartment and an AR-15 with four magazines in a vehicle belonging to his roommate, consistent with the weapon in the photograph.[^1]

The federal case, USA v. Blevins (case number 5:22-cr-00169), was filed in the [[Western District of Texas]] on April 6, 2022, on the single count of felon-in-possession-of-a-firearm. Blevins entered a guilty plea on November 28, 2023. A judge in the Western District sentenced him to 60 months (five years) in federal prison in May 2024. The prior felony that triggered the felon-in-possession charge was not specified in the available press releases.[^1][^4]

No federal terrorism or material support charges were filed in connection with InJekt Division's Telegram activities.
