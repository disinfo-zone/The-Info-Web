Claude Thirault, a petty criminal, assisted [[Marc Dutroux]] in 1993 with renovations to a basement at Dutroux's Marcinelle residence, transforming it into a hidden dungeon designed for imprisoning kidnapped girls. Thirault, hired by Dutroux under the pretense of installing a drainage system, helped construct soundproofed cells concealed behind removable plywood partitions disguised as shelving. During the work, as two high school girls passed by, Dutroux remarked to Thirault that abducting them could yield 150,000 francs by grabbing them from behind, sedating them with a drug, and securing them in a vehicle. This comment, overheard by Thirault, revealed Dutroux's intentions, prompting Thirault to report the suspicious activity to police at the time, though investigators dismissed it as unfounded.[^1]

Two days after the July 22, 1995, disappearance of eight-year-old [[Julie Lejeune]] and [[Melissa Russo]] from Charleroi, Thirault reiterated his 1993 concerns to authorities, emphasizing Dutroux's earlier statement as evidence of premeditation. Despite this, the Othello investigation led by Inspector [[Rene Michaux]] did not pursue Dutroux as a suspect until much later. Thirault's involvement remained peripheral, limited to the construction assistance and subsequent warnings, which highlighted early opportunities for intervention that were overlooked amid Dutroux's escalating crimes.[^1]

### Role in Basement Construction
Thirault's labor contributed directly to the dungeon's functionality, featuring reinforced walls, ventilation adjustments, and hidden access points that enabled Dutroux to hold victims like Julie, Melissa, [[Sabine Dardenne]], and [[Laetitia Delhez]] undetected during initial 1995 searches. Police inspected the renovations in 1993 but accepted Dutroux's drainage excuse, ignoring Thirault's report. The setup allowed for prolonged captivities, with screams from the basement during Dutroux's December 1995 auto theft incarceration—while Thirault was not present—dismissed as external noise by searching officers, including Michaux.[^1]

The dungeon's design facilitated abuse and potential video production, as evidenced by seized equipment like chloroform, chains, and a speculum returned to Dutroux's wife [[Michelle Martin]]. Thirault's post-disappearance alert linked the structure to the missing girls, but systemic delays prevented action, allowing Dutroux to abduct [[An Marchal]] and [[Eefje Lambrecks]] on August 22, 1995, while under surveillance. His testimony underscored how the basement enabled the network's operations, coordinated with [[Michel Nihoul]] via [[ASCO]] for victim supply.[^1]

### Connections and Reporting
Thirault connected primarily to Dutroux through this employment, unaware of the full criminal scope until the 1995 abductions. His reports positioned him as an unwitting informant, contrasting with accomplices like [[Michel Lelievre]] and Martin, who aided post-construction. The overlooked warnings tied into broader failures, including Justice Minister [[Melchior Wathelet]]'s 1992 parole of Dutroux despite prior rape convictions. Judge [[Jean-Marc Connerotte]]'s later probe validated early red flags like Thirault's, though his 1996 removal sparked White Marches protests against perceived cover-ups.[^1]

Thirault's role highlighted informant value in fragmented investigations, with no evidence of his deeper involvement beyond labor. His alerts, ignored amid Othello's focus on unrelated theft, exemplified institutional oversights enabling Dutroux's murders of An, Eefje, and the younger victims, whose bodies were exhumed in September 1996 from sites including Sars-la-Buissière.[^1]

### Legal Outcome
Thirault faced no charges for the renovations, as his participation predated knowledge of criminal use and stemmed from a legitimate job offer. Police did not implicate him in abductions or abuses, treating him solely as a witness whose 1993 and 1995 reports could have prevented tragedies. Unlike Lelievre and Martin, convicted as accomplices, Thirault avoided scrutiny, his testimony aiding the case buildup leading to Dutroux's August 13, 1996, arrest via Laetitia Delhez's witness plate.[^1]
