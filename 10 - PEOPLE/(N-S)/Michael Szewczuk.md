Michael Szewczuk, 19, of Leeds, West Yorkshire, was a co-founder of [[Sonnenkrieg Division]] (SKD), the British neo-Nazi accelerationist organisation that emerged from the post-proscription milieu of [[National Action]] and was modelled on [[Atomwaffen Division]] with explicit [[Order of Nine Angles]] (O9A) philosophical content incorporated into its ideology. He was sentenced at the [[Old Bailey]] on 18 June 2019 by Judge [[Rebecca Poulet QC]] to four years and three months in a Young Offenders' Institute after pleading guilty to seven terrorism offences.[^1]

### Offences

Szewczuk operated a personal account on the [[Gab]] social media platform under a pseudonym and shared administrative control of SKD's Gab page. He pleaded guilty to:

Two counts of encouraging terrorism under section 1 of the [[Terrorism Act 2006]].

Five counts of possessing documents useful to a terrorist under section 58 of the [[Terrorism Act 2000]], including the White Resistance Manual and the Al Qaeda Manual.

The Gab content Szewczuk produced and posted included an image of [[Prince Harry]], Duke of Sussex, with a gun to his head on a blood-spattered background, captioned to identify the Duke as a "race traitor" who should be shot because of his relationship with [[Meghan Markle]]. Other content glorified [[Anders Behring Breivik]], the Norwegian mass murderer responsible for the 2011 [[Utoya]] and Oslo attacks that killed 77 people, and included material calling for ethnic cleansing.[^1]

### Sentencing Remarks

Judge Rebecca Poulet QC, sentencing both Szewczuk and [[Oskar Dunn-Koczorowski]] on the same day, described the content as "abhorrent as well as criminal by reason of their clear intention to encourage terrorist acts."[^2]

### Sonnenkrieg Division and O9A

Sonnenkrieg Division was founded by [[Andrew Dymock]] after his departure from the [[System Resistance Network]] (SRN), incorporating O9A philosophy heavily into SKD's ideological framework. Szewczuk's role was as a co-founder and Gab operator. The Home Secretary proscribed SKD under the Terrorism Act 2000 in 2019, after Szewczuk and Dunn-Koczorowski had been charged but before their sentencing.
