Melissa Russo, aged eight, disappeared from her hometown of Charleroi, Belgium, on July 22, 1995, alongside her friend [[Julie Lejeune]], who was also eight years old. The two girls vanished while riding bicycles near a local school, an incident that prompted immediate police involvement but yielded no leads until the broader investigation into [[Marc Dutroux]] intensified. Dutroux, an unemployed electrician with a history of child-related offenses, had kidnapped them on orders linked to his role in a child trafficking network, holding them captive in a hidden basement dungeon at his residence in Marcinelle, where they endured starvation and abuse for over a year.[^1]

The basement, constructed with assistance from accomplice [[Claude Thirault]] in 1993 under the guise of drainage renovations, featured soundproofed cells disguised behind shelving. Surveillance began on August 10, 1995, under the Othello investigation led by Inspector [[Rene Michaux]], but officers failed to detect the girls despite screams heard during a December 1995 search while Dutroux was jailed for auto theft. Items seized included videotapes of rapes, a speculum, chains, and chloroform, all returned to Dutroux's wife [[Michelle Martin]], who later admitted feeding the captives sporadically during his incarceration.[^1]

Dutroux's early release in March 1996 for humanitarian reasons allowed him to continue operations, abducting [[Sabine Dardenne]] in May and [[Laetitia Delhez]] in August. The breakthrough came from a witness's partial license plate matching Dutroux's white van, leading to his arrest on August 13, 1996. Rescue efforts freed Sabine and Laetitia, but Julie and Melissa's bodies were exhumed on September 16, 1996, from a property in [[Sars-la-Buissière, Belgium]], showing signs of mutilation and connected video equipment suggesting exploitation for pornography. Autopsies indicated they had not simply starved but suffered severe trauma, with Dutroux later claiming in a letter to Julie's father that the kidnapping served an organized snuff orgy.[^1]

### Investigation Failures
Police under Rene Michaux overlooked critical evidence during multiple searches of Dutroux's home in December 1995, attributing basement cries to external sources despite a locksmith's confirmation of sounds from within. The Othello team, focused on auto theft, eliminated Dutroux as a suspect in the girls' disappearance in January 1996, ignoring prior reports from Thirault about Dutroux's basement modifications and abduction plans. Chloroform and rape kit items were cataloged but not examined, returned to Martin, who neglected the captives, leading to their presumed death by early 1996.[^1]

Surveillance from August 10 captured Dutroux abducting [[An Marchal]] and [[Eefje Lambrecks]] on August 22, yet operators missed the activity, allowing the older teens to be held upstairs before their murder and burial at another site. The white Mercedes vans used, registered to [[Jean-Michel Nihoul]]'s company [[ASCO]], facilitated victim transport and photography for client catalogues, linking the abductions to a profit-driven network. Nihoul's over twenty phone calls to Dutroux around Laetitia's abduction confirmed coordination, with payments traced to Dutroux's European accounts funding his seven properties.[^1]

The exhumation revealed concrete slabs over the burial site, with video equipment tied to Dutroux's computer indicating production of abuse materials sold to wealthy clients. FBI analysis of related tapes suggested elite spectators, aligning with claims of blackmail parties. Judge [[Jean-Marc Connerotte]]'s team uncovered these connections, but his removal in October 1996 after a victims' fundraiser sparked White Marches protests by 300,000 Belgians decrying cover-ups involving figures like Justice Minister [[Melchior Wathelet]], who approved Dutroux's 1992 release.[^1]
